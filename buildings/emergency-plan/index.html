Occupant Emergency Plan

This Occupant Emergency Plan (OEP) provides each person in the building a guideline of what to do should an emergency occur inside the building or outside the building.

Incident Response

Medical
  1. Report all medical emergencies to the Fire and Life Safety Coordination Center at (212) 264-5064.
  2. AFTER making the call to (212) 264-5064, the tenant or visitor may also choose to call 911 as a precautionary measure.
Flooding (Ruptured Pipe or Sprinkler Activation)

RELOCATE OR EVACUATE

  1. Notify Fire Safety Director at 212-264-0596 or Red Floor Warden Phone
  2. Take shoes, coats, purses, bags, keys
  3. Proceed to nearest stairwell
  4. Proceed to assembly point as designated by your agency
  5. Wait until dismissed or returned to work
  6. Notify GSA
Work Place Violence

LOCK DOWN

  1. Notify Fire and Life Safety Director at 212-264-0596
  2. Notify FPS 1-877-437-7411
  3. Move people not involved away from the scene
  4. Await police department
  5. If you can get person to stop
Hurricane

DO NOT COME TO WORK

  1. When a Hurricane warning is in effect
    1. Prepare offices for storm damage
    2. Close windows and blinds
    3. Disconnect all electronic equipment
    4. Disconnect and clean out refrigerators: NO FOOD SHOULD BE IN THE OFFICE
    5. Move paper files to internal storerooms away from windows
    6. Take electronic records to safe location
    7. Monitor the weather
  2. When the storm approaches
    1. Remain at Home: No one should be in the building without permission from GSA
    2. Wait until storm passes
  3. After the storm passes
    1. Check your people, call for medical assistance if needed
    2. Check your building
      1. No cracks
      2. No broken windows
      3. Structure is intact
      4. Remain where you are
      5. If building is damaged evacuate

Note: The only people in the building during a Hurricane should be GSA and security staff, everyone else should be at home until the storm passes and local government allows people back into the area!

Earthquake

If the building shakes or moves do the following:

  1. Get under a strong table or desk
  2. Wait until the shaking stops and nothing is falling before moving out from under the furniture
  3. Call for medical assistance if anyone is hurt
  4. Survey your office
  5. If only items have fallen off desks, shelves, STAY Where you are
  6. Report if the building is damaged to the Command Center:
    1. Shattered windows
    2. Cross hatching in walls (ÒXÓ in cement or cinder blocks)
    3. Ceiling or doors have collapseThe safest place in an earthquake is indoors.

NOTE: There is more to fall on you outside than insidThere is little danger of any of our facilities collapsing during an earthquake or afterwards.

Power Failure

SHELTER IN PLACE/Agency Dismissal

  1. Turn off equipment.
  2. Turn on flashlights, if needed.
  3. Check restrooms to get anyone out stuck in the dark.
  4. Check local news to see the extent of the power failure.
  5. There is no rush in a power failure to leave, roads will be jammed, mass transit will be disrupteLeave when you normally do or when the building becomes uncomfortable.
  6. If you have no place to go, shelter in place.
Civil Disturbance Outside the Building (Demonstration, Riot, Police Emergency Outside the Building, Fire in Neighborhood)

SHELTER IN PLACE

  1. Close and lock office doors and doors to the outside of the building.
  2. Shelter in Place until is safe to leave.
Hazmat (Spill Inside or Outside the Building)

SHELTER IN PLACE

Suspicious Package

SHELTER IN PLACE

  3. Move all people out of the room

Code Adam (Missing Child)
  1. Please cooperate with security officials.
  2. Look around your workspace for the missing child.
  3. If you find the missing child, bring him/her to the nearest security officer. If there is no security officer in the immediate area, you cannot leave to bring the child to the lobby or the child will not accompany you, immediately notify the nearest security officer
  4. If the child is found with someone other than the parent/guardian, use reasonable efforts to delay the departure of the person accompanying the chilDO NOT PUT YOURSELF OR OTHERS AT RISK. Notify security officials and provide a description of the person accompanying the child.
Active Shooter
  1. EVACUATE: If there is an accessible escape path, attempt to evacuate the premises. Be sure to:
    • Have an escape route and plan in mind
    • Evacuate regardless of whether others agree to follow
    • Leave your belongings behind
    • Help others escape, if possible
    • Prevent individuals from entering an area where the active shooter may be
    • Keep your hands visible
    • Follow the instructions of any police officers
    • Do not attempt to move wounded people
    • Call 911 and the FPS Megacenter when you are safe
  2. HIDE OUT: If evacuation is not possible, find a place to hide where the active shooter is less likely to find you.
    • Your hiding place should:
      • Be out of the active shooterÕs view
      • Provide protection if shots are fired in your direction (i.e., an office with a closed and locked door)
      • Not trap you or restrict your options for movement
      • To prevent an active shooter from entering your hiding place:
        • Lock the door
        • Blockade the door with heavy furniture
    • If the active shooter is nearby:
      • Lock the door
      • Silence your cell phone and/or pager
      • Turn off any source of noise (i.e., radios, televisions)
      • Hide behind large items (i.e., cabinets, desks)
      • Remain quiet
    • If evacuation and hiding out are not possible:
      • Remain calm
      • Dial 911, if possible, to alert police to the active shooter's location
      • If you cannot speak, leave the line open and allow the dispatcher to listen
  3. TAKE ACTION against the active shooter as a last resort, and only when your life is in imminent danger, attempt to disrupt and/or incapacitate the active shooter by:
    • Acting as aggressively as possible against him/her
    • Throwing items and improvising weapons
    • Yelling
    • Committing to your actions
